The idea

Price is a shadow, not a series

Most models learn price directly — and inherit all the opacity that comes with it. We invert the problem: fit the population of traders and the mechanism that clears them, then read price as output. The internals fall out as reusable signal.

Real data → Agent simulation + Learned encoder → Fair-value cloud → Explainable rules → Products
01 · GENERATIVE

Two engines, one world-model

FastPop grows thousands of trader personalities that compete on a simulated exchange. UMarketSim reads the same dashboard and produces calibrated probability clouds. They warm-start each other — simulation feeds learning, learning feeds simulation.

02 · EXPLAINABLE

Rules, not black boxes

After a simulation run, we extract what the winning traders actually did — as readable rules. "Buy what's cheap versus fair value. Don't pile onto a position you're already deep in." Nobody programmed that in. It emerged.

03 · AUDITABLE

Rigor is the product

Future data cannot leak backward. Trading costs are charged. Baselines are compared. When a hypothesis fails — our 15-minute crypto calls were a coin-flip at 49.9% — we write it down and kill it. The willingness to disprove ourselves is the whole point.

The machine

Differentiable physics, honest forward pass

Don't learn the world from scratch — learn the deviations from a mechanism you already trust. Order-book matching is the structural prior. Small neural networks fill the gap.

Simulated crowd

Market-makers, trend-chasers, bargain-hunters, institutions, noise traders — each with its own decision network. When they trade against each other, realistic fingerprints emerge: fat tails, volatility clustering, calm-then-storm dynamics. Without being told to.

Reality-matching dials

A handful of knobs — jumpiness, correlation, trend strength — auto-tune until the simulation's stylized facts match real market data. We fixed a subtle measurement bug that had been letting random luck masquerade as improvement.

Probability cloud

From recent history, the encoder produces not a single guess but a calibrated range: where something should be worth, and how confident the model is. When it says 90%, we checked — it actually means about 90%.

What we know

Built to be checked, not sold

We don't ask you to believe a number. We show you the process, the tests, and the failures. Here's an honest snapshot of where things stand today.

5 proven 11 working 2 early 5 planned

Earnings shocks are real

We measured it on real data: earnings announcements roughly double a stock's next-day swings. Board departures and shareholder votes barely register. Passed a pre-declared kill-switch test — statistically real, not luck.

Policy network redesign

Found the old trader-brain design was half-broken — 52% dead neurons. Redesigned and measured: dead neurons fell to under 1%. The new design can express market-maker behavior the old one literally could not.

We killed our own idea

49.9%

A live 30-hour experiment: 545 predictions on Kalshi's 15-minute crypto contracts. Result was a coin-flip. We wrote it down instead of hiding it. That live-scoring machine now waits for a better-suited signal.

Fair-value ranking

The main strategy — rank a basket by how cheap each asset is, lean toward bargains. Built with full trading costs and strict past-vs-future separation. Early results are promising but the edge is not yet robustly proven on real money. That's what we're testing now.

The foundation is rigorous and explainable. Most of the upside is still ahead of us — and we'd rather you see that clearly than pretend otherwise.
Where it's going

Several products, one engine

The stack isn't built for a single use case. Fair-value ranking, prediction-market arbitrage, governance data, and a strategy studio all draw from the same fitted world-model.

Working · repointing

Kalshi signal layer

Use the fair-value estimator to spot mispriced prediction-market contracts. Live scoring infrastructure is built and running — we're aiming it at longer-horizon, fair-value bets instead of 15-minute coin-flips.

Early · testing

Fair-value ranking

Sort a basket from most-underpriced to most-overpriced. Tilt toward bargains. Can learn the best way to blend model signals, not just use one by hand.

Planned

Crypto compounder

Run fair-value ranking on crypto, where trading is cheap enough that a slow, steady edge can survive the costs. Deliberately waiting for the ranking edge to prove out first.

Planned

Operator cockpit

A dashboard for daily supervision — picks, proposed trades, rolling retrains, human approval before anything executes. The bridge from backtest to real deployment.

Planned

CEO-pay governance data

Rank companies by whether executive pay tracks performance. Built on machine-readable SEC filings. No trading required — a standalone data product for ESG funds and journalists.

Vision

Strategy studio

An app where people generate, inspect, and trade strategies — explainable, not black-box. A playback viewer for simulations already exists as the first seed.

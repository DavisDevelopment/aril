The thesis

Price is a shadow, not a series

Most quantitative models study the shadow — the price series itself — and inherit all the noise and opacity that comes with it. Janus studies the thing casting the shadow: the crowd of participants whose decisions become the price. Understand the crowd, and the chart stops being a mystery and starts being an output.

01 · TWO FACES

One engine, looking both ways

Like its namesake, Janus has two faces. Agora looks inward — a living market in miniature, grown from first principles and tuned until it behaves like the real thing. Pythia looks outward — reading real markets and answering with fair value and honest confidence. Each face sharpens the other.

02 · READABLE

Answers you can argue with

Janus doesn't hand you a number and demand faith. Its conclusions come out as rules in plain language — the kind a portfolio manager can read, question, and veto. Classic risk discipline shows up in those rules on its own, without anyone writing it in. That's what earned our trust in the approach.

03 · DISCIPLINED

The bar is set before the test

Every claim Janus makes has to survive machinery built to kill it: live scoring against real outcomes, full trading costs, strict past-versus-future separation, and comparison against simple baselines. Ideas that don't clear the bar are retired in the open — which is exactly why the ones that survive mean something.

The engine

Agora and Pythia — the two faces of Janus

A market is a conversation between thousands of participants with different goals, horizons, and appetites. Janus models the conversation, not just its transcript.

Agora — the inward face

A synthetic market, populated by thousands of distinct participants — patient institutions, restless speculators, liquidity providers, opportunists — trading against each other in a world Janus controls completely. The hallmarks of real markets emerge on their own: rare violent moves, calm that turns to storm, crowding and retreat. Nobody scripts them. They emerge — which is how we know the machinery underneath is honest.

Pythia — the outward face

Pythia reads the real tape and answers the only question that matters: what is this actually worth, and how sure are we? Her answer is never a single number — it's a fair-value estimate wrapped in a confidence range that has been checked against reality. When Pythia says 90%, we've verified it means about 90%.

The dialogue between them

Agora is tuned until its synthetic markets are statistically indistinguishable from real ones — a rehearsal space where Janus can run ten thousand market histories instead of the single one reality offers. What's learned in rehearsal gives Pythia her head start on the real thing; what Pythia sees in the wild flows back to make the rehearsals sharper.

Discipline

What our green lights actually mean

Anyone can show you a backtest. We built the lie-detector before we built the product — so that when something on this page is marked proven, that word is carrying real weight.

Proven — survived pre-declared tests Working — built and running Early — promising, still being validated Planned — designed, ahead of us

The test comes before the claim

Every hypothesis gets its pass/fail bar written down before the experiment runs — so a result can't be reinterpreted into a success after the fact. Earnings events, for example, earned their place in the engine by clearing a pre-declared statistical bar on real data; ideas that sound just as plausible but didn't clear it were left out. The engine contains only what survived.

A live scoreboard, not a backtest

We built machinery that logs predictions on a regulated exchange and scores them when markets settle — a grader that cannot be argued with. In a thirty-hour live run, 545 ultra-short-horizon calls landed at 49.9%: a coin flip. We retired that idea in writing and re-aimed the same machinery at longer horizons. The discipline that kills a weak signal in thirty hours is the same discipline that makes a surviving signal worth backing.

Leakage is impossible by construction

The most common way quantitative research lies is subtle: information from the future seeps into the past, and a strategy looks brilliant right up until it meets reality. Janus's data foundation is built so that this physically cannot happen — every model, at every moment, sees only what was knowable at that moment. It isn't a policy we follow; it's a property of the architecture.

The flagship strategy, tested the hard way

Janus's core strategy ranks a whole basket of assets by how far each sits from fair value, and leans toward the bargains. It is being validated under the most hostile conditions we can construct: full trading costs charged on every trade, strict separation of past and future, and head-to-head comparison against simple benchmarks that are harder to beat than most people admit. We mark it early rather than proven because our bar for "proven" is high — and because a green light you can trust is worth more than a headline you can't.

What's proven is rigorous and explainable. Most of the upside is still unpriced — and we'd rather show you exactly where the frontier is than pretend we're past it.
The horizon

Markets are the first surface, not the last

What Janus actually learns is more general than any one market: how a population of decision-makers, each acting on partial information, drives a system toward an outcome. Exchange order books are simply the cleanest place to begin. Feed the same engine richer inputs and the world it can model — and price — widens dramatically. Every new kind of data isn't a new product to build from scratch; it's a new sense for an engine that already knows what to do with it.

→ THE HUMAN GRAPH

Who moves whom

Behind every price is a web of people — the relationships, allegiances, and overlapping histories among the decision-makers who steer companies and capital. Mapped as structure the engine can read, that web becomes a modelable force, not background noise.

→ LANGUAGE AT SCALE

Information, the moment it's information

The world announces itself in words first — filings, headlines, narrative, sentiment. An engine that ingests that flow as it breaks reacts to what's changed the instant it changes, rather than waiting for it to show up in the tape.

→ FRONTIER REASONING

The best models, in the loop

The most capable reasoning systems available, integrated as first-class participants — reading, connecting, and forming hypotheses alongside the simulation. Janus is built to put their judgement to work inside the engine, not merely beside it.

Each of these is another current feeding the same river: one cohesive engine that turns more of the world into something it can simulate, anticipate, and act on. Markets were the proving ground. The ambition is a general capital engine — and its foundation is deliberately built to grow into exactly that.

Where it's going

One engine, several products

Janus wasn't built for a single trade. The same fitted engine prices event contracts, ranks assets by fair value, and powers tools people can put their hands on.

Working

Event-market pricing

Pythia's fair-value estimates, aimed at regulated event markets — spotting contracts the crowd has mispriced and taking the good side. The live trading and scoring plumbing is already built and running.

Early · validating

Fair-value ranking

The flagship: sort a basket from most underpriced to most overpriced and tilt toward the bargains. Janus can also learn how to weigh its own signals — the strategy improves as the engine does.

Planned

The compounder

Fair-value ranking deployed where trading is cheap enough for a steady edge to compound undisturbed. Deliberately sequenced after validation — the product launches when the edge is proven, not before.

Working · prototype

Android field app

A Kotlin mobile prototype already on device: live model fitting, history playback, and an on-device Distill tab that runs the same symbolic rule extraction as the Python stack — readable rules, streamed from the engine on the phone.

Planned

Argus — the operator console

Named for the watchman with a hundred eyes: a daily cockpit showing picks, proposed trades, and model health, with a human approving every action before it executes. A static console preview and session-playback harness exist today; the full supervised deployment loop is designed but not shipped.

Vision

Janus Studio

A studio where users generate, inspect, and share strategies that explain themselves — because everything Janus produces is readable by design. Two seeds already exist: an Electron playback viewer for engine runs (umarketsim-viz) and the Android prototype above.
